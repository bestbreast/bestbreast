Is SAFELipo good for fat grafting?
May 28, 2020
SAFELipo is an excellent technique for fat extraction to do fat grafting. This is because no thermal energy is imparted to the tissues and the chances of the fat cells surviving the transfer is higher. I use SAFELipo all of the time and am very happy with it.

Who is the ideal candidate for SAFELipo?
Who is the ideal candidate for SAFELipo?
Sep 11, 2019
The ideal candidate for SAFELipo is the ideal candidate for liposuction surgery in general. Someone who is close to ideal body weight who has specific areas where contouring could be improved. SAFELipo is just a tool/technique, and the ideal candidate is the same across techniques for someone who has not had surgery before. One of the advantages of SAFELipo is that is can safely (pun intended) be used for revision surgery in patients who have had ultrasonic or laser techniques previously.

What is the purpose of having drains for gynecomastia surgery? Is it highly recommended to have drains
What is the purpose of having drains for gynecomastia surgery?
Sep 11, 2019
Thank you for your question. Drains are used to remove fluid that your body will naturally produce in an area that has had surgery so that the tissues will heal together properly and not be separated by a fluid buildup. Depending upon your surgeon and particular type of surgery, drains may be recommended. For most of my patients who have surgery for gynecomastia, we do not need to use drains. I would never say “never”, but it is pretty rare for me to find an occasion to use drains. Good luck.

Is losing your belly button part of the risk of a tummy tuck?
Sep 3, 2019
Thank you for your question. The belly button sometimes is moved, but most often “stays in place” as the other tissues move around it. In either case, the belly button usually does not lose its blood supply and does just fine. There is always a risk of losing the blood supply and the belly button skin not surviving, but this is extremely rare. This is part of our consent process, since it is a known risk.
Does SAFElipo need fat grafting procedures?
Does SAFElipo need fat grafting procedures?
Aug 5, 2019
In short – No. The reason fat grafting is not needed with the SAFELipo technique is that the last part of the process is called Fat Equalization (The “FE” in “SAFE”), which basically is micro fat grafting in the area that was treated using the small amount of residual fat in the area. So nothing is removed/replaced for the FE portion – it uses what you have locally to even out the fat.
