Should I have gone with silicone implants instead of saline?
Never Go Over 400 CC
Aug 29, 2014
I rarely suggest that a patient goes larger than 400 cc, especially if sagging and stretching is a concern of the patient. The weight of the implant can stretch the skin. I think that saline implants are perfectly fine, and you can keep the ones you have if you are not looking to spend more money on implants if you get a revision. The appearance of your breasts 8 months-1 year post-op should be much better and allow you to properly evaluate whether or not your final result is acceptable...
I am wondering if I should get a breast lift/implant before I have more children?
Most Logical Decision Is To Wait
Aug 29, 2014
What I always recommend to my patients is that they first complete their family. Once your family is complete, you can get the results you desire, and maintain the results long term. I suggest having the implants done first, and once they settle you are able to do a perfect placement of the areola complex with the lift.

I personally do not like icing. It causes vaso-constriction and creates a compromise of blood flow and possible necrosis. Heat is not better. Neither cold nor warmth has an effect on healing.

My left breast looks fuller than my right. Is this normal after 4 weeks? Will they even out?
Breast symmetry does not exist
Jul 28, 2014
This is normal for 4 weeks post surgery. Breast symmetry does not exist. Surgeons try to create symmetry. Give yourself 6 months to completely heal and see the actual results. In the meantime, you can use an upper pull bandeau to help pull down the implants or a custom form sports bra to help mold the breasts.
