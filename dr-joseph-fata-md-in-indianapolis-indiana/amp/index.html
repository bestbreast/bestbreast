Does scarring ever fade completely after a breast lift?
Breast Lift
Jun 9, 2020
Based on your photos, I would recommend a vertical breast lift which results in a "lollipop" type scar. It will result in the least amount of scar while truly lifting your breast tissue into a more attractive shape. Although scars do tend to fade over time, no surgical will ever truly disappear no matter what you have heard or read. Thais is a fact you will have to accept before proceeding with surgery. Good luck.
Doughnut breast lift. What do I risk?
Choosing a type of breast lift
Jun 9, 2020
A "donut" (periareolar) breast lift moves the nipple but not the lower portion of your breast whereas the vertical ("lollipop") lift would move both. Since you need to have the lower part of your breast lifted to have a natural, well balanced breast (with or without an implant), I would recommend the vertical technique. Also, you do not have much available fat for transfer so if you want fullness of the upper breast, you will need an implant as well.

I'd like a second opinion, is 405cc enough to meet my goals?
Choosing a cup size
Dec 19, 2019
Most women considering breast augmentation use  cup size as a way to communicate their goals. However, cup size alone is not precise enough because different manufacturers size their bras differently (Victoria's Secret is the biggest outlier) and implants are based on volume. Assuming that 405cc would fit your body's dimensions, ask your plastic surgeon to let you see how you would look using implant sizers placed in your bra. If you are happy with how you look, then cup size doesn't matter.

Can you help me decide between teardrop or round implant on Asian body?
Tear Drop Implants
Dec 3, 2019
All tear dropped shaped ( also called form-stable) implants have textured surfaces. I believe that the trend is moving away from textured surface implants because of the small but real risk of ALCL (Anaplastic Large Cell Lymphoma), which have never been reported in smooth surface implants. You should discuss this risk with your plastic surgeon before making a decision.
