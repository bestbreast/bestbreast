Above or below the muscle

Most implants are placed under the muscle these days for many benefits – better shape in the upper part of the breast, less implant visibility specially in thin women, significantly decreased capsular contracture rate, improved mammogram results as compared to above the muscle placement.

However, there are women that would benefit from above muscle placement. There is no one answer for everyone. It depends on your specific physical attributes – breast size, shape, skin tone, mobility of your breast over the muscle and whether or not you need a breast lift or not.

Going with a larger implant to stretch your skin may look good in the short term and you may avoid a lift but in the long run a larger implant will stretch your skin more and you will get even more sagging – “a rock in a sock” appearance.

If you skin tone is already causing you to have sagging, adding more volume will only worsen the situation unless you address the loose, excess skin.

If you need a lift, I would do the lift and go with a smaller implant size which will give you better long term results.

If you still have questions regarding the recommendations you have been given, go back for a second visit now that you have heard both surgeons’ opinions and let them justify their positions and why they are recommending one procedure vs. another.

Or – get a third opinion which should help you decide on the correct procedure – remember to think about the long term outcome as well as the short term.

Implant Bottoming Out

It is difficult to give you definitive advice regarding the position of your implant without examining you or seeing your picture. I would recommend you see your surgeon or a Board Certified Plastic Surgeon who can assess the fold competence and provide you with a structured plan.

If it is “bottoming out”, your fold will need to be reconstructed (much like building an internal bra) to support the tissues and bring the implant back up to where it belongs. Judging fold position by where the scar is currently is not the best way to assess the situation.

Are Breast Implants realistic for those who run long distances daily?

Breast implants are quite durable and many aggressive athletes have them. However for your comfort I would suggest smaller implants 300 cc or below and definitely have the implants placed under the muscle. In addition a firm supportive sports bra should arise be worn when running.

Will pregnancy ruin my breast implants

Your pregnancy in itself will not affect or compromise the integrity of your breast implants. Most women who have had breast implants have been able to breast feed without difficulty. Normal breast changes during and after pregnancymay include stretch marks, nipple areola enlargement , skin discolorations, and breast sag.

These are predominantly weight gain and hormonal induced changes and are not caused by your breast implants. Any breast sag or droop secondary to pregnancy can be corrected with a variety of breast lift procedures.
