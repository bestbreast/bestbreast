Can I have my 450 cc implants deflated first before determining if I should decide on a lift or explant
Preoperative Deflation
May 15, 2020
Preoperative deflation is a very useful tool prior to revision augmentation. It not only helps give a better idea if a lift is needed but also allows the tissue to rebound a little without the weight of the implant. Based on your photos a lift will be necessary. If the capsule is indeed "crunchy" or calcified it should be removed as well.

What are the costs, pros/cons, etc for CO2 laser labiaplasty?
Laser Labiaplasty
Jun 30, 2019
Using a laser has few benefits from traditional labiaplasty techniques. It's just another way to cut the skin. It's used as a marketing technique. Ask to see before and afters and discuss your specific concerns. Many different fields offer labiaplasty - Ob/Gyn, Urology, Plastic Surgeons. The key is to find someone that has the proper training and experience. Cost of traditional labiaplasty will vary by location and experience. Average in the midwest is likely around 3500-5000. 
DVT ten years ago during my first pregnancy in ‘09. No complications since. Can I safely proceed with m
TT with DVT history
Jun 30, 2019
You can likely proceed safely but I would put you on a prophylactic anticoagulant like Eliquis or Pradaxa for the first week post op. This has been shown to be safe at reducing DVT and minimizing bleeding complications. Make sure to discuss with your plastic surgeon. Abdominoplasties do have a higher risk of DVT compared to many other types of plastic surgery procedures.

Went to a consultation and PS says no need for lift if I get a big enough implant (don’t want the scars
Large implants vs. a lift
May 29, 2019
I would choose your surgery based on your final goals, not the final scars. Don't go bigger than you really want to be to avoid the lift. You'd likely have the best outcome in terms of shape, size and nipple positions with a lollipop lift and implant. An implant alone will likely leave your nipple slightly low but it would fill your upper pole. Talk this through with your surgeon and consider a second opinion.
