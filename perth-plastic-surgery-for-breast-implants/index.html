How much ptosis is too much ptosis?

Ptosis or droop/sagging of the breast is clinically defined by the relationship between the NAC (nipple areolar complex) and the IMF (inframammary fold).

As a woman ages, the breast can drop and the nipple which begins above the fold may, ultimately, descend to the level of the fold, below the fold or on some cases be facing downwards.

While it is true that implants do impart a lift this is not reliable and will not correct any significant degree of ptosis. It creates the illusion of a lift but does not impart a measurable change in nipple position.
