Pain and new lines on Breast augmentation incision 9 weeks post op. Should I be concerned?
Incision problems
Feb 19, 2019
In general there shouldn’t be new redness along the incision 9 weeks after surgery but this sometimes can happen with the sutures not dissolving as they should. I’d have your surgeon take a look to make sure there is no concern for infection. The scar tissue along the incision will be palpable at 9 weeks which will soften with time. If you use tobacco I’d be concerned about a late infection. Best of luck
Am I a candidate for tummy tuck at this weight? Or maybe even a lower body lift?
tummy tuck
Nov 11, 2018
Unfortunately, weight alone is not enough to determine if you are a good candidate for a tummy tuck. A BMI is slightly more helpful but seeing a board certified plastic surgeon for a consultation will be the best approach. Look at before and after photos as well to find photos of similar cases as your own. You would likely get much better results if you were able to lose more weight but you can discuss this with your surgeon. Best of luckJosh Olson MD
80 lb weight loss: do I need a lift or just implants?
breast lift needed
Nov 11, 2018
Great job with the weight loss. If you place implants alone the nipple will sit very lower on the breast and while in a bra or clothing you would look fine, out of clothing you may not be pleased with the appearance. A breast lift will reposition the nipple and tighten the skin around the implant for a much improved shape. Hope this helpsJosh Olson MD
If I get another lift will that give me upper pole fullness?
revision breast surgery
Sep 16, 2018
I revision lift may increase your upper pole fullness but without know your surgical history and examining you it is difficult to say. Larger implants are heavier and the weight of the implants may be more than your tissue can hold up so adding Galaflex or Strattice for additional support may be reasonable. Going with a smaller implant can also increase your upper pole fullness in the long term. Best of luckJosh Olson MD
Can tuberous breast deformity type 3 be fixed with just a lift and augmentation?
tuberous breast deformity
Sep 16, 2018
You can definitely achieve improvement in your breast shape and size with implants and a breast lift. I would recommend a textured implant in the sub muscular pocket as well as a circumareolar mastopexy to decrease the areola size and tighten the loose breast tissue around the implant. You are at a higher risk of a double bubble when lowering the fold but this tends to stretch or improve with time. Best of luckJosh Olson MD
Why is my implant of 10 years shifting?
changes after 10 years
Jun 23, 2018
Gravity and weakening breast tissue or capsule are to blame for changes over time. Nothing is permanent especially with changes such as breast feeding and pregnancy. You would likely benefit from a breast revision surgery to tighten the breast implant pocket and swap out the implants. A breast lift will also likely be needed. See a board certified plastic surgeon for more information and recommendations. Josh Olson MD
Do I need a lift or will implants be enough to give a fuller and perky look?
lift or no lift
Jun 23, 2018
You can probably achieve a good result either way. A lift may give your breast a better shape and nipple position if you are wanting a smaller implant. If you raise you arms up in the air in front of the mirror and there is no breast dropping over the breast fold an implant alone may be appropriate. Your areola will however widen with an implant so a donut lift can prevent this. Hope this helpsJosh Olson MD

Help!! 18 day PO Breast Lift with 200cc moderate profile Implants, why are my breasts burning?
Burning after breast aug
May 28, 2018
As the stretched nerves wake up it is common to have a burning sensation or hypersensitive nipples. This is nothing to be concerned about. If there is new redness along the incision and/or fevers make sure and let your surgeon know. As far as the shape is concerned you are swollen and the implant is likely going to settle. Best of luckJosh Olson MD

Do I require a tummy tuck or would I be fine with just lipo?
tummy tuck vs lipo
May 22, 2018
Liposuction will reduce the fat and flatten your abdomen but you will have overhanging skin. A Tummy tuck will give you a flatter appearance and likely a better result. You can always have liposuction first and a tummy tuck at least six months later if you don’t get the results you were hoping for. While photos don’t give us an idea of rectus weakness vs visceral fat, weight loss prior to a tummy tuck may give you optimal results. Best of luckJosh Olson MD
