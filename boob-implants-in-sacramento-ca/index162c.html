Implant Rippling

All implants ripple, whether in or out of the body. This can be seen very clearly with display implants in the surgeons office, which should’ve been shown to you. Rippling represents a loss of support of the outer shell as it interacts with the inner content.

The lower the viscosity, the more rippling. Additionally, thicker implant shells tend to have firmer, more palpable ripples, and this is why non-form stable textured implants are at higher risk for rippling. When implants are in the body, the other important factor is tissue coverage.

Dr Jonathan R. Freed Mentor Breast Implants Photos Result

The thicker your breast tissue/subcutaneous tissue layer is, the less implant rippling is either felt or seen. Knowing this, saline implants ripple the most, with the deepest and firmest defects that are most likely going to be felt and/or seen through the breast tissue.

Regular cohesive gel implants ripple less. Highly cohesive, form stable anatomically shaped implants ripple the least, which was the one of the two main purposes of their design (the other is maintenance of shape over time).

Implants that are either too big, too wide, or those that have drifted into malposition usually also ripple more.!.
