You should discuss your concerns with that surgeon in person. In general, if the nipple-areolar complex is below the fold under your breast, a variation of a lifting procedure will be beneficial to achieve an ideal aesthetic result.

Which implant is best for you?

It is common for every patient to wonder which size, type and shape of implant is best for them when there is so much information out their that seems conflicting and confusing.

This is because there is no ‘one implant fits all rule’. An obvious statement but the same implant in different scenarios of tissue elasticity (young vs older breast), planes (in front vs behind the muscle), and chest wall dimensions can alter the final look dramatically – even thought the implant may be the same. Implant right size can also be deceptive as the more important measures are the dimensions (width, projection, shape,etc) of the best implant that will fit your body shape, chest dimensions and skin envelope.

An example of implant right size being misleading is that by altering the width of an implant by 5mm (keeping its height and projection the same) can increase its volume by over 10%. My approach is to first understand what you, the patient wants. This incorporates knowing what your desired look is (cleavage, desired bra cup size, natural vs busty,etc).

This I feel is the most important step – ‘the surgeon listening to the patient’. The rest is based on my experience and expertise to get you to your goal.

Then as part of my examination, I assess your chest wall, breasts, skin tone/quality, and overall body shape. Measurements as well as clinical photos are taken.

Sizing kits and computer programs that project your final result aid in the communication process and give you an idea of size in clothing but remember that they are not accurate enough to be specific for your tissue qualities (tone, elasticity, etc) out of clothing.

Based on these factors, I then explain what implant (or implants) would suit you best to achieve your desired outcome. If there is more than one implant that will be suitable, I also use the aid of the ultimate check which is to use each suitable implant sizer during the operation and choosing the exact best that matches with your conveyed wishes.

In summary, convey to the plastic surgeon your desired outcome in as much detail as you can (they will also guide you), ensure they hear you, check all your questions are answered to your liking and they have the necessary experience and expertise to help you achieve your desired outcome. (Kevin Ho, MBBS, FRACS, Sydney Plastic Surgeon)

3D Imaging Of Your Breasts Will Help You Determine What Implant right Size Is Best For Your Anatomy & Goals

Your question is one that many patients agonize over. It is very hard to accurately visualize how a particular implant right size or shape will appear on you and how you will feel about it once it is in place. Until very recently it was really a matter of trusting that your surgeon had gotten a good read on your goals at the time of your consultation and then relying on their experience to translate that into a certain implant shape or size.

Obviously, this communication and trust are still very important but now we have a new imaging system called the Vectra Imager that takes a photo of your body, and then applies the different implants to it to give us an image of what it would actually look like. Getting this concrete visual input makes your decision making process much more accurate and reliable and helps you to be more confident with the decisions that you make regarding your augmentation. (Marie E. Montag, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)

Choosing Right Breast Implant Sizes

You have a lot of options when choosing breast implant sizes. One tip I suggest to my patients is to bring in a padded bra that gives them the look they love. That way, I can get a better sense of their goals. Next, we use sizers (which are silicone forms) to “try on” a look. When sizing patients, it is important to look at the WHOLE person: butt, hips, thighs, waists and shoulders and not focus just on the chest. What looks “stripper-esque” on a woman with a narrow frame may look womanly and balanced on a woman with wide hips. Finally, 3-D computer imaging is another tool we use to help our patients “preview” their look. (James N. Romanelli, MD, FACS, Long Island Plastic Surgeon)
