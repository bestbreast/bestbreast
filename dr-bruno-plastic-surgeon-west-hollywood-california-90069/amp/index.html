Board Certified ER Doctor performing BBLs - Any concerns?
Which type of doctor should perform your BBL
Jun 30, 2020
I would recommend a board certified plastic surgeon for your BBL procedure, not an ER doctor. They do not have the necessary training required for liposuction and fat grafting techniques. I would also recommend having the procedure performed under general anesthesia, not local.  Seek out a second or even third opinion before having your surgery.Best wishes,Dr.Bruno
Tummy tuck without muscle repair?
Tummy tuck with muscle repair
Jun 19, 2020
Most patients benefit form muscle repair during a tummy tuck, however, some men do not necessarily need the muscle repair since they may not have muscle separation. This muscle separation, called rectus diastasis, occurs mainly in women after pregnancy. If the muscles seem lax at the time of surgery, then the surgeon should go ahead with the muscle repair as it adds to the overall contour. If you are unsure, ask you surgeon again to be re-examined or seek a second opinion.Best wishes,Dr.Bruno
Is my breast skin too thin for implants? I have deep stretch marks all over them
stretch marks and breast implants
Jun 15, 2020
The stretch marks typically do not pose an issue with implants. It's really the amount of tissue underneath the skin. As long as you don't use implants that are too large (ex. >400cc) your soft tissue should be able to support the implants. Consult with an experienced plastic surgeon in person for more information and a complete exam.Best wishes,Dr.Bruno

Why does your BMI have to be considered for flank/tummy lipo and BBL?
high BMI and surgery
May 18, 2020
Patients who have BMI's (body mass index) over 30 are considered obese. This carries increased risks of pneumonia, infection, wound healing difficulties and DVT (deep vein thrombosis). It's ideal to have a BMI at or under 30 before having elective cosmetic surgery. Discuss this in more detail with your surgeon before scheduling surgery.Best wishes,Dr.Bruno
