Breast Enlargement Overview

Implants usually don’t give much of a lift

The position of the nipple rarely changes with breast augmentation. What does happen is the loose skin may be stretched and filled in by the implant. The only way to lift the nipple is with a breast lift.

You may however get enough fullness with the augmentation that you will be happy with the nipple position. A lift can always be done later if needed.

Augmentation alone will not correct sagging breasts

Breast augmentation in your particular situation will not help in fact it will make the condition worse. Not only will you not get any lift from the implant you will have an unusual shape to the breast as well.

This is called a snoopy dog nose breast. The implant will be held in a high position and your breast will sag and droop off of the implant. Your best option is with a breast lift.

Should I decide what size implants or should doctor decide?

Neither you nor your plastic surgeon chooses implant size unilaterally. The decision is made after you communicate your desired outcome, and your surgeon communicates alternatives, risks, benefits, and possibilities, not just about implant size, but placement, content of implant, surface of implant, and surgical approaches.

The surgeon may agree with you, and use your chosen device for augmentation. Your surgeon may recommend what he/she believes is best for you, or may advise you against what you desire if in reality it may not be in your best interest.

The Peri-aeolar Incision

The periareolar incision can only be considered if that areola is big enough or the implant is small enough. Large cohesive implants are almost always better placed through and inframammary fold incision. On the other hand, saline implants can almost always be placed through the periareolar incision.

Capsular contracture is always a significant concern, and the risk of this may be higher when using the periareolar incision. This incision usually heals beautifully, but if you are unlucky enough to have excess scarring, you will be looking at that scar every day.

And inframammary fold scar is out of sight and usually forgotten, even without perfect healing.

Uneven breasts

All woman have some element of asymmetry between their breasts. It can be something minor like a small volume discrepancy or slight difference in nipple position, or something more obvious like your clinical picture: more ptosis (sag) on the right, larger right breast, and higher/tighter breast crease on left.

Before you began, you should make sure that your expectations are realistic and that you will still have some element of asymmetry postoperatively. First you should decide what size you wish to be (see below for link to question/answer on choosing sizes).

In your case, you will need a larger implant on the left and a smaller one on the left. The implants will help to even out the sizes, give you more upper pole fullness, and help address the lower pole tightness.

Very early in the program.

You are a week out. I tell most of my patients that this is a four to six month program. Augmentations typically look great on the table. After surgery they rise upward, and are swollen, (and sometimes “goofy -looking”).

They will usually start to move downward at about the week three stage. Message, and/or a bandeau may help. Time is the main thing that you need here. Do tell your doctor your concerns.

Most doctors may make changes to size, but only giving time for the situation to settle and the tissues to become supple. Sometimes this can be a year after surgery. Remember each patient is different, and it is important that your doctor knows how you are feeling.

Wear comfortable bras. There is no research showing that uncomfortable bras work better. I would advise against underwire pushup bras as they rub on the wound.

Round vs. Teardrop Implants

This is a very controversial issue among plastic surgeons and patients. Round implants in Michigan are softer, and assume a teardrop shape when standing. Teardrop implants tend to feel firmer and impact the final shape of the breasts.

Currently among plastic surgeons, this controversy has led to new studies being performed. While I do often place teardrop implants, patients have to be aware the risks, such as rotation, and the extra firmness. Each patient needs to to evaluated for what type of implant is best for them.
