Textured and smooth breast implants

One of the keys to a good patient-physician relationship is good communication. Speak with your surgeon and find out the reason for the change in the plan. In general, textured implants do not offer many benefits over smooth implants.

Some surgeons believe there is a lower capsular contracture rate with textured implants. This is most true when the implant is placed above the muscle. Textured implants are also more useful when an anatomic (shaped) implant is chosen versus a round implant.

The texturing helps prevent the anatomic implant from moving around which is important since there is a specific shape to it versus a round implant. However, texturing can have some downsides such as increased palpability and higher rupture rates. In general in our office in Bloomfield Hills, we use smooth round implants for breast augmentation.

A natural feel to breasts with implants

Most breasts with implants feel more natural if they have a significant amount of breast tissue or fat around the implant. If the woman is thin, with thin tissues and not much breast volume, the implant will constitute the majority of the volume of the breast and it is very unlikely that it will feel “normal”.

The most natural feel to breasts with implants is obtained with a correct match between the volume of the implant and the existing volume of the breast tissue and fat, not to exceed a 50/50 proportion.

Fat transfer can be performed very successfully in addition to implants for a more “natural” feel, and it is currently used to treat problems like rippling, palpable implants, and thinning of the breast tissue after biopsy or other reconstructive procedures.

It is not as simple as above or below. There are actually 4 planes for placement of implants. Subglandular, subfacial, partial submuscular (duelplane), or total submuscular. Which plane is used depends on multiple factors, but the most important is how much soft tissue coverage you have.

In patients with adequate soft tissue coverage, I find the subfacial plane will give the best aesthetic result and avoid animation deformity.
