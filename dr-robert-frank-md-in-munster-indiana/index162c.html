Stretched out belly button or hernia?
You'll find out in surgery
Jan 20, 2020
It does appear that you have an umbilical hernia - something very common in the black community compared to other racial groups. Typically, those are easy to repair in surgery and require no more time in recovery. The only advice I would have is to really limit your lifting postop to avoid disrupting the repair. Like so many procedures, hernia repairs are much easier to fix the first time. Each subsequent recurrence is harder to deal with.

Am I the right candidate for a hybrid tummy tuck?
Yes, maybe even a mini
Jul 3, 2019
You appear to have a long distance between your umbilicus (belly button) and your pubic area. Patients like that, with skin redundancy limited to the lower abdomen are sometimes candidates for a mini abdominoplasty. It will likely be hard to move your umbilicus down to your tummy tuck incision which also makes leaving your belly button alone an attractive option. I'd discuss the pros and cons with a board certified plastic surgeon. Good luck.
Can abdominal fascia laxity be repaired?
It can be improved but you may not want to hear the answer
Jul 3, 2019
Often the weakness to the abdominal wall after a tummy tuck is something that has to be improved through core muscle strengthening...that is to say, working out. Through your pregnancies and surgeries, your abdominal wall has "bowed", and flattening that bowing will take strengthening your abdominal muscles. Good luck.
