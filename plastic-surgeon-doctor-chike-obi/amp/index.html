Lift and implants gone wrong!! Bad scarring and asymmetrical? Need some answers.
Plan
Aug 6, 2017
You will likely need to accept two additional surgeries to optimize your chances of success.
Surgery 1: Revision mastopexy with either implant downsize or implant removal
Wait 6 months, then:
Surgery 2: Re-augmentation (with larger implants to match your goals)

Silicone sheets are helpful in decreasing the risk if thickened, or raised scars.
Scars with increased pigment can be addressed with a number of topicals including hydroquinione or arbutin.

Will my tattoo get stretched or larger with a boob augmentation?
It’s unlikely
May 22, 2017
However it is possible to get what are called Mondor’s cords in the area where your tattoo is after breast augmentation. These are usually self-limiting and respond to warm compresses, NSAIDs. Consult with your surgeon.
What surgery would you recommend? I desperately want this sorted
Implants first
Jan 8, 2017
Implants will improve your volume asymmetry, which means your breasts will match up better when in a bra. Implants will not correct the asymmetry in nipple position and skin quantity between your breasts. A breast reshaping procedure would need to be done on the larger breast to improve these issues.

All the best.

I would like to know if I am bottoming out and if so, if its worth fixing?
Your decision
Jul 13, 2016
This is bottoming out. There are several options. To minimize your risk of recurrence, I would perform internal suture based maneuvers (capsulorrhaphy) and provide soft tissue support with an off-the-shelf biological mesh such as Strattice. The soft tissue support part is very important in my opinion. Not to talk you into smaller implants, but the smaller the devices, the lower your risk of recurrent bottoming out.
